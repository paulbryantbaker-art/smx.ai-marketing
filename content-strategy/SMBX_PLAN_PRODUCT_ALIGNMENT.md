# HOW THE PRODUCT EVOLUTION AFFECTS THE 90-DAY PLAN
## What the repo tells us that the LinkedIn content doesn't reflect yet

---

# THE BIG FINDING

The product is positioned as an **AI deal team** — not an educational platform, not a calculator, not a marketplace. The site copy, journey pages, and master spec all describe Yulia doing the actual work of an investment bank: building CIMs, modeling financing, scoring deals, drafting LOIs, managing DD checklists, coordinating closing, and running 180-day PMI.

The LinkedIn content we built is still in **"M&A thought leadership"** mode — teaching people about multiples, screening frameworks, and deal structures. That's Seed phase credibility content, and it works for Weeks 1-4. But the product transition in Weeks 5+ needs to match the DIRECTNESS of the site.

The site says: **"Yulia runs your deal. You make the decisions."**
The LinkedIn content says: **"Here's how PE screens deals."**

Those are two different voices. The second one needs to become the first one by Week 5.

---

# 5 SPECIFIC GAPS BETWEEN PRODUCT AND PLAN

## GAP 1: RAISE Journey Is Missing

The product has a full Raise journey (R0-R5): readiness assessment, financial package, investor materials, investor outreach, term negotiation, closing. The site copy describes minority equity sales, ESOP, convertible notes, revenue-based financing, cap table projections, investor deck generation.

**The 90-day plan has ZERO Raise content.** The 3-problem framework maps to Sellers, Buyers, and Deal Pros. Raise is a fourth customer problem that cuts across:

- **CEO/founders who want growth capital without giving up control** — this is a Wednesday/Executive audience
- **Business owners who don't know there are alternatives to 100% sale** — this is a Monday/Seller audience
- **PE/search funds evaluating capital raise structuring** — this is a Friday/Deal Pro audience

**Fix:** Add Raise as a recurring content angle, not a separate day. Weave it into existing days:
- Mondays (Sellers): "You don't have to sell 100% to get liquidity. Here are 6 structures most owners don't know exist."
- Wednesdays (Executives): "How a $12M EBITDA company raised $8M in growth equity without giving up control."
- Fridays (Deal Pros): "The raise vs. sell decision: when clients should take minority capital instead of a full exit."

Suggest: 1 raise-focused post per week, rotating across personas. That's 13 posts over 13 weeks — enough to establish the capability without diluting the core sell/buy content.

## GAP 2: Content Is Educational, Product Is Operational

**What the site says Yulia does:**
- Builds the valuation (not "explains valuation")
- Generates the CIM (not "what's in a CIM")
- Models the financing (not "how financing works")
- Scores the deal (not "how to screen deals")
- Drafts the LOI (not "what should be in an LOI")
- Manages the DD checklist (not "due diligence best practices")
- Preps the negotiation (not "negotiation tips")
- Coordinates the close (not "closing process overview")

**What the LinkedIn content does:** Teaches and educates. "How PE picks the winner." "How to sell at the top of the EBITDA range." "What happens when SBA fails."

**The gap:** Our Seed content (Weeks 1-4) is correctly educational — establish credibility before showing product. But from Week 5 on, the transition needs to shift from "here's how the process works" to **"here's what Yulia just DID on a real deal."**

**Fix for Weeks 5-8:**
- Instead of: "Here's how a CIM works" → "Yulia generated a 30-page CIM from a conversation. Took 30 minutes. Here's page 1."
- Instead of: "How to screen 20 businesses" → "Yulia scored 5 deals this morning. Two passed. Three were dead before I read a page."
- Instead of: "The SBA math on a $30M deal" → "Yulia modeled the complete capital stack in 60 seconds. Base case, downside, break-even."

The content stops TEACHING and starts SHOWING. Screenshots. Output examples. Before/after. The product IS the content.

## GAP 3: The IB Comparison Is Approved But Unused

The Platform Reference and Competitive Intelligence docs explicitly approve IB comparison framing for How It Works and Pricing pages. The 5 pillars doc says "smbx.ai does 90% of what an investment bank does."

**The LinkedIn content never makes this comparison.** Not once in 260 posts.

**The line is clear:**
- ✓ "Here's everything an IB does on a $30M deal. Yulia does 90% of it."
- ✓ "A CIM takes a boutique IB 4-8 weeks. Yulia generates one in 2 hours."
- ✗ Never: "Fire your broker." "Cheaper than your advisor." "No broker needed."

**Fix for Weeks 5+:** The IB comparison becomes a recurring content angle — not attacking advisory, but showing the SCOPE of what Yulia does. This is Pillar 1 (Yulia Does the Work) with teeth.

Post example: "Here's the deliverable list from a $30M sell-side IB engagement: valuation, CIM, buyer list, outreach strategy, LOI analysis, DD coordination, working capital model, closing checklist. Yulia generates every one of these. The 10% she doesn't do? You pick up the phone and negotiate."

This post isn't anti-broker. It's pro-capability. It says "we operate at this level" without saying "and your broker doesn't."

## GAP 4: Advisor/Broker Content Is Too Narrow

The product has a specific Advisor journey: "80 hours/month on data and docs → 12 hours/month. 3-4 deals turned away → zero. 68 hours back = 4-6 more deals." First 3 client journeys free. White-label everything.

**The 90-day plan treats advisors as a Friday-only deal pro audience.** But brokers are the HIGHEST-LTV customer segment — one broker adoption = $999/month enterprise subscription + client flow.

**Fix:** Advisors deserve more than Fridays. Add broker-specific content to:
- Tuesday or Thursday (Buyer day): "A broker I work with screened 12 deals for a client in an hour. Previously: 3 weeks."
- Wednesday (Executive): "The brokerage that added an AI deal team. Capacity went from 12 deals to 30. Same headcount."

The advisor pitch isn't "Yulia replaces you." It's "Yulia gives you 68 hours back every month."

## GAP 5: Post-Close / Integration Is Underleveraged

The product has a full PMI journey (PMI0-PMI3): Day Zero protocols, 30-day stabilization, 60-90 day assessment, 90-180 day optimization. The Deal Intelligence Narrative describes this in detail: "The deal doesn't end at closing. Day 1 after the wire hits is when the real work starts."

**The 90-day plan barely touches post-close.** It appears in a few Thursday buyer posts as "operator transformation stories" but the INTEGRATION journey and the PMI intelligence are invisible.

**Fix:** Post-close content serves TWO audiences simultaneously:
- **Buyers:** "You just closed. Here's what the first 30 days need to look like."
- **Sellers:** "75% regret selling. The buyers who DON'T regret it had a 180-day integration plan."

This also differentiates from every competitor. Nobody else covers post-acquisition. It's Pillar 4 (Full Lifecycle) in action.

# REVISED CUSTOMER QUESTIONS (From 3 to 5)

The original framework:
1. How much can I get? (Sellers)
2. How to screen/close/run? (Buyers)
3. How to manage at scale? (Deal Pros)

**Updated to match the product:**
1. **How much is it worth — and how do I increase value?** (Sellers + Executives)
2. **How do I find, buy, and create value?** (Buyers + Acquisition Entrepreneurs)
3. **How do I raise capital without giving up control?** (Founders + Owners)
4. **How do I manage a portfolio of deals faster?** (Brokers + Search Funds + PE)
5. **How do I protect value after close?** (Post-acquisition + Integration)

These map directly to the four journeys (Sell, Buy, Raise, Integrate) plus the advisor use case. Every post answers one of these five.

# WHAT CHANGES IN THE CALENDAR

## Weeks 1-4 (Seed): Minimal changes
Educational content is still right for credibility. Small tweak: add 1 Raise post per week and 1 post-close post per week by replacing the weakest existing post for that day.

## Weeks 5-8 (Soft Open): Significant shift
Content transitions from TEACHING to SHOWING. Instead of "here's how the process works," it's "here's what Yulia just did." The IB comparison enters the frame. Branded terms + product capabilities are demonstrated, not explained.

Key new angles:
- "Yulia generated this CIM in 30 minutes" (screenshot/mockup)
- "Here's the complete deliverable list from a $30M sell-side engagement. Yulia does all of it."
- "Yulia scored 5 listings. 2 passed. Here are the scores."
- "You don't have to sell 100%. Yulia models 6 capital structures."

## Weeks 9-13 (Push + Flywheel): Major shift
The content IS the product. Screen recordings. User stories. Before/after comparisons. The IB comparison is explicit. The advisor pitch is direct. Pricing is revealed as the punchline.

Key new angles:
- "A broker ran 3 client CIMs through Yulia this week. Total time: 90 minutes. Previous: 120 hours."
- "Founding member raised $8M in growth equity using Yulia's investor materials. Never gave up control."
- "Day 1 after a $35M acquisition. Yulia built the integration plan from the deal data."
