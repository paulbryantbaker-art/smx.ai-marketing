# THE THREE BRANDED TERMS

## The Baseline™
**What it is:** Your real adjusted earnings — the number a buyer actually uses to write a check. Not revenue. Not net income. Not the tax return. Adjusted owner earnings (SDE or EBITDA) with every legitimate add-back identified, documented, and defensible.

**Who uses it:** Brokers presenting valuations to clients. Sellers understanding their real number. PE assessing normalized EBITDA.

**How Yulia delivers it:** Multi-methodology valuation with 22 deterministic formulas. Add-back schedule documented line by line. Seven-factor quality score.

**LinkedIn usage:** "Know your Baseline before anyone else does." "What's your client's Baseline?"

---

## Blind Equity™
**What it is:** The gap between what the tax return shows and what the business actually earns. The add-backs and adjustments that exist in the financials but nobody has identified — because the person preparing the financials was optimizing for taxes, not enterprise value.

**Who uses it:** Brokers finding hidden value for seller clients. PE identifying additional EBITDA in targets. Sellers discovering their business is worth more than they thought.

**The data:** Average Blind Equity across founding member analyses: $1.1M per deal. At 5-6× multiple: $5.5M-$6.6M in enterprise value.

**Most common sources:** Above-market related-party lease (64% of deals). Excess owner compensation (58%). Non-recurring professional fees (44%). Personal expenses through business (41%).

**LinkedIn usage:** "Yulia found $2.6M in Blind Equity in the first conversation." "Your client's accountant minimizes taxes. Yulia finds the Blind Equity."

---

## The Rundown™
**What it is:** 7-dimension deal scoring. 60 seconds. Pursue or pass before spending a dollar on diligence. Scored 1-100. Every deal that enters the pipeline gets a Rundown score before anyone picks up the phone.

**The 7 dimensions:** Concentration, margins, revenue quality, owner dependency, management depth, financial integrity, scalability.

**Who uses it:** Search fund operators screening thousands of deals. PE associates evaluating CIMs. Brokers qualifying listings before taking mandates. Buyers comparing opportunities.

**The data:** 62% of deals scored below 40 (hard pass). 18% scored above 70 (pursue). Deals below 40 that were pursued anyway: 78% died in diligence at an average cost of $34K.

**LinkedIn usage:** "The Rundown: 7 dimensions. 60 seconds. Pursue or pass." "Scored 12 deals. 9 dead. Total time: 11 minutes."
