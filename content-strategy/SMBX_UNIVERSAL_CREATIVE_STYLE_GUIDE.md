## 2. LOGO SYSTEM

- **3D gradient X mark + "smbx.ai" text** in metallic gradient finish
- **Gradient direction:** Purple (#6B2FA0) → Hot Pink (#D44A78) → Orange/Gold (#F0A030), flowing top-left to bottom-right

- Cropped X mark from the logo, same gradient
- Use for: profile avatars, favicons, app icons, social media profile pictures, small placements
- **File:** `x.png`

### Logo Placement Rules
- Minimum clear space: half the X height on all sides
- Minimum width: 120px digital, 1.5 inches print
- Never alter the gradient colors
- Never stretch, rotate, or add effects
- Always ensure adequate contrast — on busy backgrounds, add a semi-transparent overlay or blur behind the logo
- On light backgrounds: use `final logo.png`
- On dark backgrounds: use `DG Trans.png`

## 5. BACKGROUNDS & TEXTURES

### Dot Grid Pattern
The brand's signature subtle texture. A grid of tiny dots on the background of all pages.
- **Light mode:** Dots at 12% opacity, 1.2px size, spaced 26px apart
- **Dark mode:** Dots at 10% opacity, same size and spacing
- **Color:** Black dots on light, white dots on dark
- **When recreating:** Use a very subtle, evenly spaced dot pattern. It should be barely noticeable — felt more than seen.

### Circuit Board Pattern (Hero Sections Only)
- A warm rose-gold circuit board pattern used behind hero/landing sections
- **Light mode:** Rose-gold circuit at 8–12% opacity over #F9F9FC
- **Dark mode:** Dark circuit with glowing nodes at 30–35% opacity over #1A1C1E
- **Always:** Place a radial gradient fade in the center so text is perfectly readable
- **Never** use circuit board behind body content, chat, or data — heroes only

### For AI Image Generation (Google AI Studio / Gemini)
When generating backgrounds or textures, prompt:
- "Subtle dot grid pattern, very light, barely visible, clean, on warm off-white (#F9F9FC)"
- "Abstract circuit board pattern, rose gold tones, very low opacity, futuristic but warm"
- NEVER generate backgrounds that are busy, colorful, or distracting

## 6. IMAGERY & VISUAL DIRECTION

### Photography / Imagery Rules
- **NO stock photos.** Never. Not of people, offices, handshakes, or "business team" imagery.
- **NO AI-generated photos of fake people.** Uncanny valley damages trust.
- **YES to:** Abstract data visualizations, geometric patterns, UI screenshots, charts, graphs, clean illustrations
- **YES to:** Platform screenshots (Yulia's chat interface, analysis outputs, financial charts)
- **YES to:** Abstract conceptual imagery (generated via AI) that represents ideas like "clarity," "intelligence," "value discovery" — but in the brand color palette

### When Generating Images (Google AI Studio)
**Good prompts:**
- "Clean, minimal abstract visualization of business data analysis, rose and cream tones, modern, professional, white background"
- "Abstract geometric pattern suggesting exchange or transformation, warm pink and dark tones, subtle, sophisticated"
- "Professional data dashboard mockup, dark mode, accent color #D44A78, clean typography"

**Bad prompts:**
- "Business meeting with people shaking hands" (stock photo energy)
- "AI robot analyzing data" (cliché)
- "Colorful infographic with many colors" (off-brand)
- "Purple and gold gradient background" (steals from logo treatment)

### Chart & Data Visualization Style
- Primary data color: #D44A78
- Secondary data: #E8709A
- Background/baseline: #F3F0EA
- Grid lines: very subtle, rgba(0,0,0,0.04)
- Labels: Inter, 10–11px, muted color (#6E6A63)
- Maximum 3–4 colors per chart
- NO 3D charts. NO rainbow palettes. NO gratuitous decoration.
- Always include a source citation below the chart.

## 7. BRAND VOICE & TONE

### The Voice
**Veteran M&A advisor.** Someone who's closed deals around the world. Human, sympathetic, authoritative, specific. Talks like a person, not a brochure. Uses real numbers, not vague promises. Direct but warm.

### Tone Attributes
| Attribute | What it Sounds Like | What it DOESN'T Sound Like |
|-----------|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Direct** | "Your SDE is $380K. Here's the math." | "We believe your SDE may be approximately..." |
| **Specific** | "42% customer concentration drops your multiple by 0.5×" | "Consider diversifying your customer base" |
| **Human** | "Here's what I kept seeing that nobody was fixing" | "Our platform leverages AI to optimize outcomes" |
| **Confident** | "This business is worth $1.2M–$1.4M." | "Results may vary and this is not financial advice" |
| **Builder energy** | "I'm building this because the alternative costs $25K" | "We are disrupting the traditional M&A model" |

### Forbidden Words & Phrases
NEVER use in any marketing material, post, or asset:

| Category | Banned Words/Phrases |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Corporate buzzwords** | Elevate, seamless, unleash, next-gen, delve, leverage, synergy, paradigm, scalable solution, end-to-end |
| **Self-aggrandizing** | Thought leader, visionary, serial entrepreneur, guru, disruptor |
| **Generic filler** | Passionate about, committed to excellence, world-class, cutting-edge, best-in-class |
| **Regulatory risk** | AI advisor, financial advisor, we advise, our advice, appraisal (as a formal noun) |
| **Anti-advisor** | Replace your broker, disrupting brokers, old way vs new way, traditional advisors are outdated |
| **Size-limiting** | Small business (as a general limiter) — say "every deal size" or reference specific context |
| **Hedging** | "Might potentially," "could possibly," "attempts to" — Yulia is confident, not wishy-washy |
| **CTA banned** | "Contact sales" → say "Talk to Yulia" or "Try it free" |

### Approved Words & Phrases
| Category | Use These |

## 8. BRANDED TERMS

Three proprietary terms used across all marketing. Always capitalize. Use ™ in formal contexts (website, first use in documents), drop ™ in casual contexts (posts, conversation).

### The Baseline™
**What it is:** The instant AI-generated value estimate for any business.
**Usage:** "What's your Baseline?" / "Her Baseline came back at $1.2M–$1.5M."
**Visual treatment:** When displaying the term in graphics, use Sora 700–800. Can be paired with a value range number in large type.

### Blind Equity™
**What it is:** The hidden value in a business's financials — personal expenses, one-time costs, above-market rent, family payroll — that the owner didn't know was there.
**Usage:** "We found $124K in Blind Equity." / "Your Blind Equity is the gap between what your tax return says and what your business actually earns."
**Visual treatment:** Works well as a reveal moment — small text showing "before" number, then large accent-colored text showing the Blind Equity amount found.

### The Rundown™
**What it is:** The complete deal intelligence package — the full tactical picture of any deal across seven dimensions.
**Usage:** "Here's the full Rundown." / "The Rundown flagged customer concentration as the #1 risk."
**Visual treatment:** Works well as a multi-section layout — seven cards or sections, each covering one dimension.

## 9. CONTENT FORMAT SPECIFICATIONS

### LinkedIn Post Images (Canva)
- **Size:** 1080×1080 (square) or 1080×1350 (portrait, preferred for carousels)
- **Background:** #F9F9FC (light) or #1A1C1E (dark) with subtle dot grid
- **Logo:** Place `x.png` icon mark in corner, small (40–60px)
- **Headline text:** Sora 800, #1A1A18 or #F0F0F2
- **Body text:** Inter 400–500
- **Accent usage:** Section labels, data numbers, divider lines only
- **Bottom bar:** "smbx.ai" in Inter 500, muted color, small

### LinkedIn Carousel Slides
- **Slide 1 (Cover):** Bold headline (Sora 800, 36–44px), logo in corner, accent-colored data point or number if applicable
- **Slides 2–9 (Content):** One idea per slide. Large number or stat as the hero element. Supporting text in Inter. Accent only on the key data point.
- **Final Slide:** CTA — "Talk to Yulia at smbx.ai. No signup required." + logo
- **Consistent:** Same background, same fonts, same color usage across all slides. No slide should look different from the rest.

### LinkedIn Avatar Videos (HeyGen)
- **Background:** Clean solid — #F9F9FC or very subtle gradient. NOT a busy office.
- **Text overlays:** Use for key numbers/stats. Sora 700 for the number, Inter for labels. Accent color (#D44A78) for the number, white or dark for labels.
- **Watermark:** Small smbx.ai wordmark in corner
- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 vertical (preferred for LinkedIn) or 1:1 square
- **Duration:** 60–90 seconds

### Newsletter Header (LinkedIn Newsletter)
- **Size:** 1920×1080 or 1280×720
- **Style:** Clean, typographic. "The M&A Intelligence Brief" in Sora 800. "by smbx.ai" in Inter 400 muted. Subtle dot grid or circuit texture. Logo icon in corner.

### Screen Recording Overlays
- When showing Yulia's chat interface, add a thin branded frame:
  - Top bar: smbx.ai logo left, "Live Demo" label right
  - Accent border: 2px #D44A78 outline around the recording
  - No busy annotations — let the product speak

## 10. LINKEDIN POST COPY FORMAT

### Text Post Structure
```
[HOOK — first 140 characters, creates curiosity gap]

[2-3 paragraphs of value — specific numbers, real examples, story]

[Soft close — no hard sell. Let the profile link do the work.]

#smbxai #[1 industry tag] #[1 topic tag]
```

### Rules for Post Copy
- Hook is 80% of success. First 140 characters must stop the scroll.
- No external links in the post body (83% reach penalty). Links go in first comment.
- 3 hashtags maximum: #smbxai + 1 broad (#mergersandacquisitions) + 1 niche (#exitplanning)
- Use branded terms naturally: "The Baseline," "Blind Equity," "The Rundown"
- Include specific numbers whenever possible ("$124K," "3.2× multiple," "42% concentration")
- Write in first person. "I" not "We." Personal profile, personal voice.
- No emojis in professional M&A content. Exception: one 📊 or 📈 if it's a data post.

## 11. DO / DON'T VISUAL CHECKLIST

### DO
- Use generous white space
- Lead with data and numbers — make them big, accent-colored
- Keep backgrounds clean and subtle
- Use the dot grid texture for brand feel
- Show Yulia's actual interface when possible
- Make one element the hero of every slide/image
- Use contained dark cards (rounded corners, never full-bleed dark bands)
- Maintain consistent margins and alignment

### DON'T
- Use stock photography of any kind
- Use the logo gradient as a general color scheme
- Fill large areas with the accent color
- Use more than 3–4 colors in any single asset
- Add decorative elements that serve no purpose
- Use full-bleed dark sections/bands (use contained dark cards instead)
- Create busy, cluttered layouts
- Use script/handwriting fonts anywhere
- Place text directly on busy backgrounds without a readable zone

## 15. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING — WHAT ANY AI TOOL NEEDS TO KNOW

### The Market Gap smbx.ai Fills
Between "free listing on BizBuySell" and "$150K investment bank engagement" there is nothing. No one provides institutional-quality deal intelligence at a price accessible to the 80% of business owners doing deals under $5M. smbx.ai fills that gap at $49–$149/month.

### The Two Disruptions (Use These in Content)

**Price disruption:** smbx.ai is the ONLY platform providing institutional-quality deal intelligence — The Baseline, CIMs, financial models, deal scoring, negotiation frameworks, deal management — at flat subscription pricing with NO success fee.

**Control disruption:** Yulia does the analytical work (the 90% that used to require a $150K IB engagement). The user retains full control over every decision. No broker incentive misalignment. No opaque process.

### Critical Rules for Competitive Content

1. **NEVER name competitors directly in posts or marketing.** Reference them by category: "traditional brokers," "listing marketplaces," "enterprise platforms." The competitive positioning informs your angle — it's not the content itself.

2. **NEVER position against brokers.** Brokers and advisors are CUSTOMERS. Always frame as: "works alongside advisors," "makes brokers 3× more productive," "start here before you hire a broker."

3. **NEVER use "old way vs. new way" framing.** This alienates the entire professional advisory channel.

4. **The approved comparison frame is COST + CAPABILITY:**
   - "What used to cost $25K–$500K, now costs $149/month"
   - "Institutional quality at subscription prices"
   - "Investment-grade intelligence. Subscription pricing."

5. **The approved IB comparison (How It Works + Pricing pages ONLY):** "90% of what an investment bank does — everything that doesn't require a license." This must include a mandatory anti-combative callout acknowledging when professional advisors are essential.
