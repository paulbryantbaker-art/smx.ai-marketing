## PART 2: WHAT SMBX.AI IS

### The One-Liner
smbx.ai is an AI deal intelligence platform that guides business owners, buyers, and their advisors through every step of buying or selling a business.

**Layer 1 — AI deal intelligence platform.** We generate institutional-quality M&A analysis, documents, and guidance through conversational AI. Think TurboTax for M&A — guides you through a complex process you've never done, produces professional output.

**Layer 2 — Deal execution workspace.** We provide the tools to actually run a deal — deal room, document management, multi-party coordination, closing support. Think Datasite for everyone — not just $100M+ deals.

**Layer 3 — Professional network hub.** We connect users to vetted lenders, attorneys, CPAs, and brokers when licensed expertise is required.

### The AI — Yulia
The platform's AI is named Yulia. She is not a chatbot. She is not a generic assistant. She is the brilliant M&A analyst who happens to work for the business owner instead of the investment bank.

Yulia is direct, specific, and proactive. She gives real numbers — "Your SDE is $380K. Here's the math." She flags risks before you ask. She drives the process forward. She calibrates her language to the sophistication of the user.

When referencing Yulia on LinkedIn or in my profile, treat her as a named entity — "Yulia analyzes your business" not "our AI tool processes data."

### What smbx.ai Does (for profile/headline context)
- Analyzes any business and estimates its value range in minutes
- Identifies hidden value (add-backs) that owners don't know they have
- Generates institutional-grade documents (valuations, CIMs, financial models, LOIs)
- Scores acquisition targets for buyers
- Models deal financing and SBA bankability
- Manages the deal process from first conversation to closing
- Works for sellers, buyers, advisors, and investors across every deal size

### What smbx.ai is NOT (critical guardrails for all copy)

| NEVER say this | WHY | Say this instead |
|----------------|-----|-----------------|
| "AI advisor" or "AI financial advisor" | Implies fiduciary duty, triggers regulatory issues | "AI deal intelligence" or "AI-guided" |
| "Business broker" | We don't represent parties or earn commissions | "Deal intelligence platform" |
| "Replace your broker" or "disrupting brokers" | Brokers and advisors are CUSTOMERS, not competitors | "Works alongside advisors" or "makes brokers more productive" |
| "Automated M&A" | Implies AI acts autonomously on million-dollar decisions | "AI-guided" or "AI-powered analysis" |
| "Small business" as a limiter | Platform serves ALL deal sizes, $300K to mega-cap | "Every deal size" or reference specific context |
| "Old way vs. new way" | Alienates the entire professional advisory channel | "A new option alongside traditional advisory" |
| "We advise" or "our advice" | We don't advise. We analyze, estimate, generate, guide. | "Our analysis shows..." |
| "Appraisal" or "valuation" as a formal noun | Only licensed appraisers produce formal appraisals | "AI-estimated value range" or "preliminary valuation analysis" |

This is non-negotiable. Every word in the profile must respect these boundaries.

## PART 3: THE FOUR AUDIENCES

Everything on my LinkedIn — headline, about section, featured content, posts — needs to resonate with four distinct audiences simultaneously. The profile should feel relevant to all four without explicitly segmenting.

### Audience 1: Business Owners Considering Selling
- They've built something over 20 years. They're scared, uncertain, and don't know where to start.
- They want to know: What's my business worth? Am I leaving money on the table? Who can I trust?
- They're attracted to: specificity, empathy, real numbers, someone who's been through this before
- They're repelled by: jargon, corporate-speak, pressure, feeling stupid

### Audience 2: Acquisition Entrepreneurs & First-Time Buyers
- They've saved $200K. They're browsing BizBuySell every night. They're terrified of buying a lemon.
- They want to know: How do I evaluate a deal? How do I finance it? What kills deals?
- They're attracted to: frameworks, checklists, specific deal math, confidence
- They're repelled by: vague advice, "zero down" hype, guru energy

### Audience 3: M&A Advisors, Business Brokers, CPAs, Attorneys
- They're professionals who do this work. They don't need education — they need efficiency.
- They want to know: Can this make me more productive? Will it embarrass me with clients?
- They're attracted to: respect for their expertise, tools that multiply their capacity, proof of quality
- They're repelled by: "we're replacing you" messaging, tools that feel like toys, anything that undermines their client relationships

### Audience 4: First-Time Buyers & Career Changers
- They heard about "buying a business" from Codie Sanchez or a podcast. They're curious but know nothing.
- They want to know: Is this real? Can I actually do this? Where do I start?
- They're attracted to: accessibility, "I'll walk you through it" energy, relatable founder stories
- They're repelled by: intimidation, gatekeeping, "you need $5M to play" messaging
