# 2. BRANDED TERMS

Three proprietary terms deployed across all marketing. Always capitalize. Use ™ in formal contexts, drop in casual posts.

## The Baseline™
**What it is:** The instant AI-generated value estimate for any business. The first number Yulia gives you. Your starting point — everything builds from here.

**How it sounds:**
- "What's your Baseline?"
- "Her Baseline came back at $1.2M–$1.5M."

## Blind Equity™
**What it is:** The hidden value in a business's financials — personal expenses, one-time costs, above-market rent, family payroll — that the owner didn't know was there. The gap between what the tax return says and what the business actually earns.

**How it sounds:**
- "We found $124K in Blind Equity."

**What it is:** The complete deal intelligence package — seven dimensions of analysis. Industry structure, regional economics, financials, buyer landscape, deal architecture, risk assessment, and forward signals. Everything you need, nothing you don't.

**How it sounds:**

- "The Rundown flagged customer concentration as the #1 risk."
- "Before you make an offer, get The Rundown."

# 4. LINKEDIN STRATEGY & ALGORITHM

## The 2026 LinkedIn Algorithm — What Actually Matters

**Posts are tested on 2–5% of your network first.** If that sample engages in the first 60 minutes, the post gets wider distribution. If not, it dies.

**Comment quality > like quantity.** A post with 50 comments outperforms a post with 500 likes. The algorithm now uses NLP to evaluate whether comments are substantive (10+ words, on-topic).

**Dwell time is tracked.** How long someone spends reading your post = quality signal. PDF carousels = highest dwell time (people click through pages).

**External links = 60% reach penalty.** Never put links in the post body. If you must share a link, first comment only.

**PDF carousels are the #1 format in 2026.** They maximize dwell time, keep people on-platform, and outperform text, images, and even video in many cases.

**Brand consistency matters more than any single post.** The algorithm rewards accounts that repeat distinctive visual/verbal elements across content. Your branded terms, your voice, your visual style — consistency IS the strategy.

## The Group Strategy

**Why groups, not personal feed:** With 300 connections, your personal posts reach ~10 people. The PE/M&A group has 240K members. You don't need followers to reach them — you need membership.

**How group algorithms work:** Same signals as main feed but narrower audience. Relevance to group topic, comment velocity in first hour, thread depth, dwell time. No external links.

**Public vs Private groups:** Public group posts CAN appear in the main feed. Private group posts are visible only to members. Most M&A groups are private — but that's fine because those members ARE your customers.

## Five Post Formats That Win in Groups

**1. Contrarian Take** — Challenges conventional wisdom. Generates the most comments because people argue. "Revenue multiples are the most dangerous metric in SMB M&A."

**2. Data Drop** — Specific market intelligence professionals can use. Gets saved and referenced. "HVAC companies in DFW are trading at 5.2×–5.8× SDE right now."

**3. PDF Carousel** — #1 performing format. Maximizes dwell time. Stands out visually against text posts.

**4. "What Would You Do?" Scenario** — Real anonymized deal scenario. Generates deepest comment threads. "Real scenario: $1.8M pest control, 38% concentration, asking $1.5M..."

**5. "I Was Wrong" Story** — Vulnerability + lesson = credibility. "For 10 years I told sellers never to share their number first. I was wrong."

## Content Adaptation Rule
Same insight, different angle per group. Never copy-paste the same post.

**Example insight: "Owner compensation adjustments change value"**
- PE/M&A group → "The SDE normalization problem: why 60% of sub-$5M valuations are wrong."
- Small Business Network → "If you pay yourself less than market rate, your business might be worth more than you think."
- Executive Suite → "I audited a $3M company. Owner paying himself $80K. Market rate: $180K. That gap changed the value by $320K."
- SearchFunder → "Before you make an offer, recalculate SDE with proper owner comp adjustments."

## Golden Hour Protocol (First 60 Min After Posting)
1. Post at 8–10 AM or 12–2 PM (Tue–Thu)
2. Reply to every comment immediately
3. If <3 comments after 30 min, share to personal feed
4. Reply substantively, ask follow-up questions to generate threads
5. 8 hours later: comment on your own post with an addendum (reactivates it)
